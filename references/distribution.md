# Distribution

Great products without distribution die. In a research pass, distribution deserves the same rigor as the product itself. "How will we reach users?" is a product question, not a marketing afterthought.

## The one-line distribution test

> *Name three specific places your first 100 users will come from, and why each channel works for this exact user.*

If you can't answer, the plan is incomplete. Add a distribution question to the clarifying pass.

## Channel-fit patterns by user segment

Use as a starting point — not a shortcut past thinking.

### Tier-2/3 cities, emerging markets, consumer
- **WhatsApp (and WhatsApp Business API)** — where conversations already happen
- **Regional YouTube / short-video creators** — trust transfers faster than search
- **Referral + peer word-of-mouth** — families decide together
- **Android Play Store ASO** — search-native; lean heavy on localized reviews
- **Cold channel to avoid:** SEO-heavy blogs (low ROI for this audience), Twitter/X (not where they are)

### US / urban consumer
- **SEO on high-intent long-tail** — if the category has search volume
- **Instagram / TikTok creators** — especially for anything lifestyle, creator, hobbyist
- **Reddit** — for hobbyist tools with active subreddits
- **Paid social** — usually wasteful in early stage; use only after product-market fit signal

### B2B SaaS — PLG / self-serve
- **SEO on problem-framed keywords** (how do I X in Y tool?)
- **Content / thought leadership** — long-form that ranks + converts
- **Integration partners** — be listed in the marketplace of the tool you complement
- **Product Hunt** — launch-day spike; not a durable channel
- **Community / Slack / Discord** — where the target user hangs out

### B2B SaaS — sales-led
- **Founder-led outbound to named accounts** — list the 50 logos
- **Conferences and trade shows** — in boring categories still the best channel
- **Partnerships with system integrators**

### Developer tool
- **HN, GitHub, Reddit r/programming or framework-specific subreddits**
- **Show HN / launch posts** on the right platforms
- **Open-source with a commercial wedge** — popular free tool with paid team/cloud tier
- **Dev influencers + podcast appearances**

### Creator / content tool
- **Meta / TikTok creators who make content about making content** (meta loop)
- **Discord communities** — creators live here
- **Affiliate programs with creator-educators**

### Marketplace / two-sided
- **Supply-side first, almost always** — build density in one geography
- **Concierge acquisition for side 1** — personal outreach, no scale
- **Subsidies / incentives** — early on, to cold-start liquidity

## Channel economics to estimate

For each plausible channel, estimate before picking:

- **CAC (customer acquisition cost)** — back-of-envelope: time × rate + paid spend per converted user
- **Reach** — realistic volume per week when the channel is "on"
- **Payback period** — months of ARPU to recover CAC
- **Scalability** — does the channel break at 10x volume?
- **Durability** — will it still work in 12 months?

A cheap channel that doesn't scale can be fine for MVP; a scalable channel with 6-month CAC payback is fine for v1. Don't pretend otherwise.

## Signals of a broken distribution plan

- Vague bullets: "social media, blog, SEO"
- One channel only — no fallback
- No founder-action step this week
- Assumes a product with zero users will spread by virality (it won't)
- Budgets paid acquisition but there's no proof of unit economics yet

## Output in `plan.md`

Add a `Distribution` subsection inside Phase 1 (MVP) and Phase 2 (v1). Each phase lists:

1. **Primary channel** — the one that matters this phase
2. **Backup channel** — if primary stalls
3. **Founder actions this week** — concrete steps
4. **CAC estimate** (rough — order of magnitude is fine early)
5. **Signal to switch channels** — what would cause you to pivot away

Distribution shares real-estate with the product in the plan. Not an appendix.

# Clarifying Questions — Tiered Bank

Pick questions matched to tier (T1/T2/T3) and idea type. Never dump all questions. Group by three buckets: **Who and Pain**, **Scope and Wedge**, **Constraints and Goals**.

Every good question shares two properties: (a) the answer changes what you build, (b) it's concrete enough that a vague answer is obviously vague. Avoid "what problem are you solving?" — it invites platitudes.

---

## T1 / S1 — Simple idea, first-time builder

Target: 4–6 questions. Plain English. No frameworks.

### Who and Pain
1. Who is the one person you're building this for — a specific real person you can name, or yourself? Describe them in one paragraph (what they do, what their day looks like).
2. Describe the last time you (or this person) hit the problem this idea solves. What happened? What did you do instead?
3. On a scale of "mild annoyance" to "makes me want to throw my laptop," how painful is this?

### Scope and Wedge
4. If this tool could only do ONE thing really well, what would it be?
5. What would make you/them say "this is magic" the first time using it?

### Constraints and Goals
6. How much time do you want to spend building this? A weekend, a month, "until it's done"?

---

## T2 / S2 — Moderate idea, technical hobbyist or junior PM

Target: 8–12 questions. Introduce frameworks with one-line definitions.

### Who and Pain
1. Describe your target user in one sentence — role, context, pain.
2. Walk me through the last time they hit this pain. What did they do? What workaround did they use? What did that workaround cost them (time, money, frustration)?
3. How often do they hit this pain — daily, weekly, quarterly?
4. Who already has a relationship with this user and is *not* solving this problem? (Signal: potential distribution partners or acquirers.)

### Scope and Wedge
5. What's the one Job-to-be-done (what the user is "hiring" this product to do) you're going after first?
6. What does the current workaround look like? (Be specific: "they copy-paste from X to Y in a spreadsheet every Monday.")
7. If someone described your product in one sentence to a friend, what would you want them to say?
8. What's the 10x better angle — speed, cost, quality, delight, ease, or something else?

### Constraints and Goals
9. What does success look like in 3 months? 12 months?
10. What's your honest time/money budget?
11. Are there constraints you're working inside (platform, tech stack preferences, team size)?
12. Anything you're explicitly *not* trying to do? (Non-goals are diamonds — they unlock focus.)

---

## T3 / S3 — Complex idea, founder or senior builder

Target: 12–18 questions. Full terminology.

### Who and Pain
1. Define the target segment (firmographic + behavioral). Size estimate and method.
2. Describe the primary JTBD — functional, emotional, social components.
3. What's the "pain × frequency × urgency" picture? Where on the stack of user priorities does this sit?
4. What existing behaviors or tools will you be displacing? (Your competitor is often a spreadsheet or a habit, not a company.)
5. Who are the "super-users" — the 10% of users who will love this 10x more than the rest?

### Scope and Wedge
6. What's your wedge — the thin initial slice that earns the right to expand?
7. What's the moat thesis — why will this be defensible after 100 imitators? (Network effects, data moat, switching cost, brand, proprietary distribution?)
8. What's the "why now" — what's changed in the last 1–3 years that makes this possible now but wasn't before?
9. Describe the core loop: what causes a user to come back tomorrow?
10. What's the monetization hypothesis? Willingness-to-pay evidence?
11. What does the positioning look like against the top 3 competitors?
12. Any unfair advantage you personally bring — relationships, expertise, assets?

### Constraints and Goals
13. What does the Month 1, Month 6, Month 12, Year 2 success state look like? Define leading and lagging metrics for each.
14. What's the capital plan? (Bootstrap, pre-seed, seed, revenue-financed?)
15. What are the hard constraints (regulatory, technical, contractual, personnel)?
16. What are the explicit non-goals?
17. What would you need to see to kill this? (Kill criteria per phase.)
18. Who are 5 potential design partners / first customers you could reach this week?

## Question types by idea type

Add these to the base set based on what kind of thing they're building.

### SaaS / B2B
- Who is the buyer vs the user? Are they the same?
- What's the sales motion — self-serve, sales-led, product-led?
- What's the integration surface (which tools must you plug into to be adopted)?

### Consumer app
- What's the core daily/weekly loop?
- What's the sharing/viral mechanic, if any?
- What's the cold-start strategy (how does user #1 get value before user #2 exists)?

### Marketplace / two-sided
- Which side is harder to acquire — supply or demand? Why?
- What's the strategy for the chicken-and-egg problem?
- What's the liquidity metric (the threshold where the marketplace starts to "work" locally)?

### AI wrapper / AI-first product
- What's the specific model-capability inflection you're exploiting?
- What's the durable moat when the base model improves? (Proprietary data, workflow, distribution?)
- What's the fallback when the model is wrong?

### Dev tool / infra
- Who champions this inside a team (individual dev, platform lead, CTO)?
- What's the adoption path — local tool → team tool → org tool?
- What's the "aha" moment in under 10 minutes?

### Hardware / physical
- What's the BOM + unit economics sketch?
- What's the manufacturing path — off-the-shelf, contract manufacturer, custom?
- What's the certification/regulatory path?

### Content / creator / media
- What's the content production system (not the artifact — the system)?
- What's the distribution edge (which platform, which algorithm, which community)?
- What's the monetization path (direct, ad, sponsor, affiliate, product)?

---

## Meta-rules

- If the user's brief already answers a question, skip it — don't ask them to repeat themselves.
- If an answer unlocks or blocks a big chunk of work, ask early and wait.
- Never ask more than 18 questions. If you want to, write a better skill.
- Good questions beget good answers. If you find yourself asking vague questions, slow down and make them concrete — name a specific scenario, reference a specific time, force a number.
